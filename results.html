Question: When was the first TV invented?

Best rated answer:
The earliest version of the CRT was invented by the German physicist Ferdinand Braun in 1897 and is also known as the "Braun" tube It was a cold-cathode diode, a modification of the Crookes tube, with a phosphor-coated screen. In 1907, Russian scientist Boris Rosing used a CRT in the receiving end of an experimental video signal to form a picture. He managed to display simple geometric shapes onto the screen, which marked the first time that CRT technology was used for what is now known as television.

Our analysis for the project:

Doctor Turk is unique because we provide financial incentives to workers by paying them to answer questions.
Our motivation behind this was that it would motivate workers to answer questions and provide more comprehensive responses.
We initially set out to test the effects of pay grade and geography.



Using all of our question/answers and answer ranking data, we generated the following pie chart:


The chart shows two main things:
1. That the crowd is in fact better at answering questions than a single person
2. The specific amount of monetary incentive was not a predictor in the quality
of the answer. However, overall, paying workers versus not paying them results in better answers.
